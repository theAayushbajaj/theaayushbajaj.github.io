Hello! My name is on the top btw.

I am a 21 year old who is fascinated by the fact that few mathematics equations govern the whole process of you reading this and me writing this.
You got it right, I am an engineer with a degree in Information Technology from Maharaja Agrasen Institute of Technology.This gives absolutely no information of what I am as person and how my brain functions.

So, I'll start with what I do and what I like. I started coding when 15 years old but that was only building logic flow-chart and executing some math function and bunch of if-else statements (sadly I was not a prodigy) but nevertheless things went uphill after that, took C++ in my high school and pretty much sorted by sorting and searching skills. That's when I developed genuine liking for computer science after actually experiencing the power a programmer possesses. I literally had no plans of pursuing CS as my graduating course, I wanted Aeronautics, glad that didn't happend because I would probably suck at it.

Got admission in a Tier-2 college blah blah blah. Cut to my 2nd year where things actually took leap. On my friends advice I started practicing and learning Python and oh boy that was one heck of an advice. Python took me to whole new world of Data Science (although there can other routes to that). But things suddenly started making sense because this time mathematics was involved. I started learning algorithms and statistics/probability, started building things,identifying problems and putting out solutions, participating in communities and contributing to open-source.
One such problem was Social Sentiment Analysis, although a bunch of solutions already existed but none was in its true form quite useful. So me alongwith my few friends took it as a challenge and built a complete end-to-end system where a company/person/gov can come and track users sentiments on their products/schemes and how social media is reacting to something and what is the swing of it. A bunch of technologies were used like Deep Learning,Web and Natural Language Processing. This project won us the Smart India Hackathon of 2018 as Ministry of Information and Broadcasting were looking something similar to this.
After that I did some personal projects mainly involved in research, you can find that on my github profile.
Did some internships too which were an amazing experience.

Currently I do freelance development for few companies and participate in kaggle and AnalyticsVidhya Competitions.
Connect with me if you want to know more (that was just 1%)
